An aged tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire in the center of the room, flickering with a dim glow. The tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, spread and diffused throughout the space. In the very middle of the room stood a large, round table, its surface scarred and weathered. At its center sat a small mantel clock, its intricate patterns ornate, ticking away in the silence. Surrounding the table were ten individuals, each dressed in worn and dusty clothes, their faces smudged with grime. Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all deeply asleep. Beside them stood a man in a black suit, a goat-headed mask covering his face. His gaze pierced through the battered mask, fixed intently on the ten sleepers. The clock on the table chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing to "twelve." From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled. At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awakened. As they regained consciousness, they looked around in bewilderment and then at each other with confusion. It seemed none remembered how they had come to be there. "Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have been sleeping before me for twelve hours." The man's attire was eerie, startling everyone in the dim light. His mask appeared to be made from a real goat's head, many of its hairs yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled together. Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze. Every gesture he made exuded not only the unique smell of a goat but also a faint odor of decay. A man with tattooed arms stared blankly for a few seconds before realizing the absurdity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the goat-head, "Who... are you?" "I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands cheerfully, as if he had long prepared an answer. A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and moments later, his expression grew grave. Strange—this room was truly bizarre. There were no doors, only walls on all sides. In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were all sealed, yet a table stood in the center. How, then, had they arrived? Had they been brought in first, and then the walls were built afterward? Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling all bore intersecting lines, dividing them into large squares. Another thing that troubled Qi Xia was the goat-head's phrase "nine of you." No matter how he counted, there were ten people seated around the table, and with the goat-head himself, eleven in total. What did "nine" mean? He reached into his pocket and, unsurprisingly, found his phone missing. "There's no need for introductions," a cool-voiced woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you've detained us for over twenty-four hours, which constitutes illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used as evidence against you." As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed dust off her arm, as if being dirty bothered her more than being imprisoned. The woman's words sobered the others. Whoever this was, kidnapping ten people alone had already crossed the line of the law. "Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted the train of thought. He looked slowly at the cool-voiced woman and asked, "We've all just woken up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, cutting to the point. The woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table. "The clock here shows twelve, but I have a habit of sleeping late. I last checked the time at home and it was already twelve. That means we've been detained for at least twelve hours," she replied.
