An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire in the center of the room, casting a dim, flickering glow. The tranquil atmosphere spread through the space like ink dripping into clear water.

At the heart of the room stood a large, mottled round table, its surface worn and scarred. In the middle of the table sat a small mantel clock, intricately patterned, ticking away. Around the table sat ten individuals, each dressed in shabby clothes, their faces smudged with dust. Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all sound asleep.

Beside them stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-head mask. His gaze pierced through the worn mask, observing the ten with keen interest. The clock on the table chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing at "twelve." From somewhere far beyond the room came a deep, resonant bell.

At that moment, the ten men and women around the table slowly awakened. As they regained consciousness, they looked around in confusion, then at each other with bewilderment. None could recall how they had arrived here.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have been asleep before me for twelve hours."

The man's bizarre attire startled everyone under the dim light. His mask seemed made from a real goat's head, its fur yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled. Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze. His every movement exuded not only the goat's characteristic musky scent but also a faint odor of decay.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before realizing the absurdity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the goat-head, "Who... are you?"

"I am sure you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands excitedly, as if he had been ready with an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and moments later, his expression grew solemn. Strange, this room was too strange. There was no door, just walls on all sides. In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were sealed, with only a table placed in the center. How had they gotten here? Had they been brought in first, and then the walls built around them?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling all bore crisscrossing lines, dividing them into large squares. Another thing that caught his attention was the goat-head's use of "nine of you." No matter how he counted, there were ten people around the table, plus the goat-head himself, making eleven in the room. What did "nine" mean?

He reached into his pocket and, as expected, found his phone missing.

"There is no need to introduce yourself," a cold female voice addressed the goat-head. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you have detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used against you." As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arm, as if being imprisoned bothered her less than being soiled.

Her words sobered the group. Whoever this was, daring to kidnap ten people alone had crossed the legal boundary.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted. He looked at the cold woman and asked, "We just woke up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was calm but sharp.

The woman calmly pointed at the clock on the table. "The clock shows twelve, but I have a habit of staying up late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours."
