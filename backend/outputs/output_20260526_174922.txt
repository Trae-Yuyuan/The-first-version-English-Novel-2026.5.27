An old tungsten filament lamp hung from the ceiling by a black wire, casting a dim, flickering glow. The serene atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, spread and permeated the room.

At the center of the room stood a large round table, worn and mottled with age. On the table rested a small, intricately patterned clock, its ticking echoing through the space. Surrounding the table sat ten individuals of varied attire, their clothes somewhat tattered and their faces smudged with dust. Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all sound asleep.

Beside them stood a man silently, wearing a goat-head mask and a black suit. His gaze pierced through the worn mask, fixing intently on the ten sleepers with keen interest.

The clock on the table chimed as both the minute and hour hands pointed to "twelve." From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled. At that same moment, the ten men and women around the table slowly awakened. As they regained consciousness, they looked around in bewilderment, then exchanged puzzled glances. Clearly, none remembered how they had come to be there.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have slept before me for twelve hours." The man's bizarre attire, illuminated by the dim light, startled everyone. His mask appeared to be made from a real goat's head, its fur yellowed and matted into tangled clumps. Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze. Every gesture he made exuded not only the distinct musky scent of a goat but also a faint, rotting odor.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before realizing the absurdity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the goat-head, "You... who are you?"

"I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands cheerfully, as if he had long prepared an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and after a moment, his expression grew serious. Strange—this room was exceedingly odd. There were no doors; all four sides were walls. In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were entirely sealed, with only a table placed in the center. So how had they arrived here? Had they been brought in first, and then the walls were built afterward? Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling were all marked with intersecting lines, dividing the surfaces into large squares.

Another thing that caught Qi Xia's attention was the goat-head's use of "nine of you." No matter how he counted, there were ten people seated around the table, plus the goat-head himself, making eleven in total. What did "nine of you" mean? He reached into his pocket and found, as expected, his phone had been taken.

"There's no need for introductions," a cold, clear-voiced woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you have detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting illegal confinement. Every word you say now will be recorded and used as evidence against you." As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arms, as if being dirty bothered her more than being imprisoned.

The woman's words sobered the group. Whoever this person was, daring to kidnap ten people alone had clearly crossed the legal line.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white lab coat interrupted the train of thought. He looked slowly at the cold woman and asked, "We all just woke up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, but it hit the mark.

The woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table and replied, "The clock here shows twelve o'clock, but I have a habit of sleeping late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was also twelve o'clock. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours." Having said that, she fell silent.
