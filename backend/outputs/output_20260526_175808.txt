An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire at the center of the room, casting a dim, flickering glow.

The tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, was spreading and permeating through the room.

At the heart of the room stood a large round table, its surface worn and mottled with age. On the table sat a small, intricately patterned clock, its hands ticking away the seconds.

Around the table sat ten individuals of varied attire, their clothes somewhat shabby, their faces smudged with dust.

Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all sound asleep.

Beside these ten, a man wearing a goat-headed mask and a black suit stood silently.

His gaze pierced through the weathered goat mask, fixing intently on the ten with keen interest.

The clock on the table chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing to "twelve."

From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled.

At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awoke.

As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in bewilderment, then exchanged puzzled glances.

None seemed to recall how they had come to be there.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I'm delighted to meet you here. You have been asleep before me for twelve hours."

The man's costume was eerily bizarre, startling everyone in the dim light.

His mask seemed to be made from a real goat's head, its fur yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled together.

Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze.

Every gesture he made not only exuded the unique, rank smell of a goat but also a faint, decaying odor.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally grasping the absurdity of the situation. He hesitantly asked the goat-head, "You... who are you?"

"I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands excitedly, as if he had long prepared his answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and moments later, his expression grew grave.

Strange. This room was truly strange.

There was no door; all four walls were solid.

In other words, the room's sides, ceiling, and floor were entirely sealed, with only a table placed in the center.

If so, how had they gotten here?

Had they been brought in first, and then the walls were built around them?

Qi Xia looked around again. Whether it was the floor, the walls, or the ceiling, all were crisscrossed with lines, dividing the surfaces into many large squares.

Another detail that troubled Qi Xia was the goat-head's mention of "nine of you."

No matter how you counted, there were ten people seated around the table. Including the goat-head himself, there were eleven people in the room.

What did "nine" mean?

He reached into his pocket and, unsurprisingly, found his phone had been taken.

"There's no need for introductions," a cold, clear-voiced woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop what you're doing immediately. I suspect you've detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting unlawful confinement. Every word you say now will be recorded and used as incriminating evidence against you."

As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arms, as if she hated being dirtied even more than being imprisoned.

The cold woman's words sobered the group considerably. Whoever this was, daring to kidnap ten people alone had already crossed the line of the law.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white lab coat interrupted the others' thoughts. He looked slowly at the cold woman and asked, "We've all just woken up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, cutting straight to the point.

The cold woman calmly pointed at the clock on the table and replied, "The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of sleeping late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours."

With that, she finished speaking.
