An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire at the center of the room, casting a dim, flickering glow.

A tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, spread and diffused throughout the space.

In the middle of the room stood a large, round table, its surface worn and mottled with age. At its center sat a small desk clock, its intricate patterns ornate, ticking away the seconds.

Seated around the table were ten individuals, each dressed in different attire. Their clothes appeared somewhat shabby, and their faces were smudged with dust.

Some lay slumped on the tabletop, others leaned back in their chairs—all were deeply asleep.

Standing silently among them was a man wearing a goat-headed mask and a black suit.

His gaze pierced through the worn goat mask, fixed intently on the ten people with keen interest.

The desk clock chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing to "twelve."

From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled.

At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awoke.

As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in confusion, then exchanged uncertain glances.

It seemed none of them remembered how they had come to be there.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have been asleep before me for twelve hours."

The man's attire was utterly bizarre, startling everyone in the dim light.

His mask appeared to be made from a real goat's head, many of its hairs yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled together.

Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze.

Every gesture he made not only exuded the distinct musky scent of a goat but also carried an underlying odor of decay.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before realizing the oddity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the goat-head, "Who... are you?"

"I believe you all share this question, so allow me to introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands cheerfully, as if he had long prepared an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and after a moment, his expression grew solemn.

Strange—this room was exceedingly strange.

There was no door; all four walls were solid.

In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were entirely sealed, with a table placed incongruously at its center.

So how had they gotten here?

Could it be that they were brought in first, and then the walls were built afterward?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling all bore crisscrossing lines, dividing the surfaces into many large squares.

Another thing that troubled Qi Xia was the goat-head's mention of "nine of you."

No matter how he counted, there were ten people seated around the table. Adding the goat-head himself, that made eleven in the room.

What did "nine" mean?

He reached into his pocket and, unsurprisingly, found his phone was gone.

"There's no need to introduce yourself," a cool, aloof woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you have detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used as evidence against you."

As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed at the dust on her arms, as if she disliked being dirtied more than being imprisoned.

The aloof woman's words sobered the group considerably. Whoever this was, daring to kidnap ten people alone had already crossed legal boundaries.

"Wait..." A middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted everyone's train of thought. He looked slowly at the aloof woman and asked, "We've all just woken up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, hitting the nail on the head.

The aloof woman casually pointed to the desk clock on the table and replied, "The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of sleeping late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours."
