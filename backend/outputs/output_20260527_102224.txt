An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire in the center of the room, casting a dim, flickering glow. The tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, was spreading and permeating through the space. At the heart of the room stood a large round table, worn and mottled with age, upon which a small, intricately patterned mantel clock ticked away. Surrounding the table sat ten individuals of varied attire, their clothes somewhat shabby and their faces smudged with dust. Some lay slumped on the tabletop, others leaned back in their chairs, all fast asleep. Beside them stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-head mask, his gaze piercing through the weathered mask with keen interest fixed on the ten.

The clock on the table chimed as its minute and hour hands simultaneously pointed to twelve. From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled. At that same moment, the ten men and women around the table slowly awakened. As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in confusion, then exchanged puzzled glances. It seemed none recalled how they had come to be there.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have been slumbering before me for twelve hours." The man's bizarre appearance, under the dim light, startled the group. His mask seemed crafted from a real goat's head, its fur yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled together. Two hollows were cut out for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze. Every gesture he made exuded not only the unique muttony odor of a goat but also a faint, rotting stench.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before realizing the absurdity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the goat-head, "Who... are you?"

"I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands cheerfully, as if he had long prepared an answer. A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and after a moment, his expression grew grave. Strange—this room was utterly bizarre. There were no doors, only walls on all sides. In other words, the room's perimeter, ceiling, and floor were sealed, yet a table stood in the center. How, then, had they arrived? Had they been brought here first, and then the walls were built?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling were all crisscrossed with lines, dividing the surfaces into large squares. Another thing that troubled him was the goat-head's mention of "nine of you." No matter how he counted, there were ten people around the table, and with the goat-head, eleven in total. What did "nine" mean? He reached into his pocket and, as expected, found his phone had been taken.

"There's no need for introductions," a cold woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you've detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting unlawful detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used against you." As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arm, as if being dirty bothered her more than being imprisoned. Her words jolted the others into alertness. Whoever this was, kidnapping ten people alone had crossed the legal line.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted, looking at the cold woman. "We've all just woken up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, hitting the nail on the head. The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table. "The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of staying up late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours." She finished speaking.
