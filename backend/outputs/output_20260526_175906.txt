An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire in the center of the room, flickering with a dim glow. The tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, was spreading and suffusing through the space. In the very middle of the room stood a large, mottled round table, its surface worn and battered. At the table’s center sat a small clock with intricate patterns, ticking away steadily. Around the table were seated ten individuals of varied attire, their clothes somewhat shabby and their faces smudged with dust. Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all deeply asleep. Beside them stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-headed mask. His gaze pierced through the worn mask, fixing intently on the ten with keen interest. The clock on the table chimed as the minute and hour hands both pointed to twelve. From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled. At that moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly stirred awake. As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in bewilderment, then exchanged puzzled glances. Evidently, none remembered how they had come to be there.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have slept before me for twelve hours." The man’s bizarre attire, under the dim light, startled everyone. His mask seemed crafted from a real goat’s head, its fur yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled together. Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze. Every gesture he made exuded not only the peculiar smell of a goat but also a faint, rotting odor. A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally grasping the absurdity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the goat-head, "Who... are you?" "I trust you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands excitedly, as if he had long prepared an answer. A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and after a moment, his expression grew grave. Strange—this room was exceedingly odd. There was no door, only walls on all sides. In other words, the room’s surroundings, ceiling, and floor were all sealed, with a table placed inexplicably at its center. How, then, had they arrived here? Could it be that they were brought in first, and the walls were built afterward? Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling alike were crisscrossed with lines, dividing them into large squares. Another thing that caught Qi Xia’s attention was the goat-head’s reference to "nine of you." No matter how he counted, there were ten people seated around the table. Including the goat-head himself, that made eleven in the room. What did "nine of you" mean? He reached into his pocket and, as expected, found his phone had been taken.

"There’s no need for introductions," a cold woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you have detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used as evidence against you." As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arms, as if she hated being dirtied more than being imprisoned. Her words sobered the group considerably. Whoever this was, kidnapping ten people alone had already crossed the line of the law. "Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted everyone’s thoughts. He looked slowly at the cold woman and asked, "We all just woke up. How do you know we’ve been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, but it hit the mark. The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table. "The clock here shows twelve o’clock, but I have a habit of staying up late. When I last looked at my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we’ve been detained for at least twelve hours," she said.
