An old tungsten filament lamp, suspended by a black wire in the center of the room, flickered with a dim glow.

The tranquil atmosphere spread through the room like ink dripping into clear water, seeping outward.

In the very middle of the room stood a large, round table, weathered and scarred with age. At its center sat a small desk clock, intricately patterned, ticking away the seconds.

Around the table sat ten individuals, each dressed in ragged, dust-stained attire. Their faces were smeared with grime.

Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all lost in deep slumber.

Beside them stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-headed mask. His gaze pierced through the worn mask, fixed intently on the ten.

The clock on the table chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing to "twelve."

From somewhere far beyond the room came a low, resonant bell.

At the same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awoke. As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in confusion, then exchanged bewildered glances.

Clearly, none remembered how they had come to be here.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am pleased to meet you here. You have slumbered before me for twelve hours."

The man's garb was truly eerie; under the dim light, it startled everyone.

His mask appeared to be made from a real goat's head, its fur yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled together.

Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze.

Every gesture he made exuded not only the goat's characteristic musky scent but also a faint, rotting odor.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally grasping the absurdity of the situation. He hesitantly asked the goat-head, "Who... are you?"

"I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you." The goat-head waved his hands cheerfully, as if he had long prepared an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and moments later, his expression grew grave.

Strange. This room was truly strange.

There was no door—only walls on all sides.

In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were all sealed, with a table placed inexplicably in the center.

If so, how had they gotten here?

Could it be that they were brought in first, and then the walls were built afterward?

Qi Xia looked around again. Every surface—floor, wall, and ceiling—was crisscrossed with lines, dividing them into large squares.

Another thing that bothered Qi Xia was the goat-head's mention of "nine of you."

No matter how you counted, there were ten people seated around the round table. Including the goat-head himself, that made eleven in the room.

What did "nine" mean?

He reached into his pocket; as expected, his phone had been taken.

"There's no need for introductions," a cold woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop this behavior immediately. I suspect you have detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting unlawful detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used against you."

As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arm, as if she hated being dirtied more than being imprisoned.

Her words sobered the group. Whoever this was, kidnapping ten people alone had already crossed the line of the law.

"Wait..." A middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted the train of thought. He looked slowly at the cold woman and asked, "We've all just woken up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, hitting the mark.

The cold woman calmly pointed at the clock on the table. "The clock here points to twelve. But I have a habit of sleeping late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we've been imprisoned for at least twelve hours."

She finished speaking.
