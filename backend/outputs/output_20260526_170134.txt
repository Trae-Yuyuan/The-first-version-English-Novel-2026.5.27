An old tungsten filament lamp hung from the ceiling by a black wire, casting a dim, flickering glow.

A tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, spread and diffused through the room.

In the center of the room stood a large, round table, weathered and worn. On the table sat a small, intricately patterned clock, ticking away.

Around the table sat ten individuals, each dressed differently. Their clothes appeared somewhat shabby, and their faces were smudged with dust.

Some lay slumped on the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all lost in deep slumber.

Standing quietly beside them was a man wearing a goat-head mask and a black suit.

His gaze pierced through the worn goat-head mask, fixating on the ten with keen interest.

The clock on the table chimed as the minute and hour hands both pointed to twelve.

From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled.

At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awakened.

As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in confusion, then exchanged puzzled glances.

It seemed none of them remembered how they had come to be there.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have slept before me for twelve hours."

The man's attire was utterly bizarre, startling everyone in the dim light.

His mask appeared to be made from a real goat's head, with much of the fur yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled together.

The mask had two hollowed-out eyeholes, revealing his cunning eyes.

Every gesture he made not only emitted the distinct musky scent of a goat but also a faint, rotting odor.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally realizing the absurdity of the situation. He hesitantly asked the goat-head, "Who... are you?"

"I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said cheerfully, waving his hands as if he had long prepared an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and after a moment, his expression grew solemn.

Strange. This room was truly strange.

There was no door, and the walls enclosed it on all sides.

In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were all sealed, with only a table placed in the center.

So, how had they gotten here?

Had they been brought in first, and then the walls were built around them?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling were all crisscrossed with lines, dividing the surfaces into large squares.

Another thing that troubled Qi Xia was the goat-head's mention of "nine of you."

No matter how you counted, ten people sat around the round table. Including the goat-head himself, there were eleven people in the room.

What did "nine of you" mean?

He reached into his pocket and, as expected, found that his phone had been taken.

"There's no need to introduce yourself," a cold woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop what you're doing immediately. I suspect you've detained us for over twenty-four hours, which constitutes illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used as evidence against you."

As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arms, as if being dirty bothered her more than being imprisoned.

The cold woman's words sobered everyone up. Whoever this person was, kidnapping ten people alone had already crossed the legal line.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted the group's train of thought. He looked slowly at the cold woman and asked, "We all just woke up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and firm, but sharp.

The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table and replied, "The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of staying up late. When I last checked the time at home, it was already twelve o'clock. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours."
