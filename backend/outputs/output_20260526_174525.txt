An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire in the center of the room, flickering with a dim glow.

The tranquil atmosphere spread through the room like ink dripping into clear water.

In the center of the room stood a large round table, weathered and mottled, with a small ornate clock on it, its intricate patterns ticking away.

Around the table sat ten individuals in various attire, their clothes somewhat worn and their faces dusted with grime.

Some lay slumped on the table, others leaned back in chairs, all fast asleep.

Beside these ten stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-head mask.

His gaze pierced through the shabby mask, fixated on the ten with keen interest.

The clock on the table chimed as the minute and hour hands aligned at "twelve."

From a distant place beyond the room came a low, resonant bell toll.

At that moment, the ten men and women around the table slowly awoke.

As they regained consciousness, they looked around in bewilderment and then at each other in confusion.

It seemed no one remembered how they had arrived.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I'm pleased to meet you here. You've been asleep before me for twelve hours."

The man's bizarre attire startled everyone in the dim light.

His mask appeared to be made from a real goat's head, many hairs yellowed and blackened, matted together.

The mask had two hollow eyeholes, revealing his cunning eyes.

His movements exuded not only the distinct smell of a goat but also a faint scent of decay.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before realizing the oddity, hesitantly asking the goat-head, "Who... are you?"

"I'm sure you all have that question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands excitedly, as if he had long prepared an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head, quickly surveying the room. After a moment, his expression grew grave.

Strange—this room was too strange.

There was no door; all four walls were sealed.

In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were entirely enclosed, with only a table in the center.

How, then, had they gotten here?

Had they been brought in and the walls built afterward?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling all had crisscrossing lines, dividing them into large squares.

Another thing that bothered Qi Xia was the goat-head's phrase "nine of you."

No matter how he counted, there were ten people around the table, plus the goat-head, making eleven in the room.

What did "nine of you" mean?

He reached into his pocket; as expected, his phone was gone.

"There's no need to introduce yourself," a cold-voiced woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop this immediately. I suspect you've detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting unlawful detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used against you."

As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arm, as if being imprisoned bothered her less than being dirty.

Her words sobered the group. Whoever this was, kidnapping ten people alone had crossed the legal line.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted, looking at the cold woman. "We've only just woken up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady but sharp.

The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table. "The clock points to twelve, but I have a habit of staying up late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. That means we've been detained for at least twelve hours," she replied.
