An old tungsten filament lamp, suspended by a black wire at the center of the room, flickered with a dim glow. The tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, spread and permeated the space. In the middle of the room stood a large, worn round table, its surface mottled with age. At the center of the table sat a small, intricately patterned clock, ticking away. Around the table, ten individuals of varied attire sat, their clothes somewhat tattered and their faces dusted with grime. Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in chairs, all fast asleep. Beside them stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-head mask. His gaze pierced through the worn mask, fixing intently on the ten sleepers. The clock on the table chimed as the minute and hour hands simultaneously pointed to "twelve." From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled. At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awakened. Gradually regaining consciousness, they first looked around in bewilderment, then glanced at each other with confusion. It seemed none remembered how they came to be there. "Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have slept before me for twelve hours." The man's bizarre attire, under the dim light, startled everyone. His mask appeared to be made from a real goat's head, its fur yellowed and matted, tangled together. Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze. Every gesture he made exuded not only the goat's distinctive gamey smell but also a faint odor of decay. A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally grasping the absurdity of the situation. He hesitantly asked the goat-head, "Who... are you?" "I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said cheerfully, waving his hands as if he had long prepared an answer. A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head, quickly surveying the room. After a moment, his expression grew serious. Strange, this room was too strange. There were no doors, only walls on all four sides. In other words, the room's perimeter, ceiling, and floor were sealed, with a table placed inexplicably at the center. How, then, had they gotten in? Could it be that they were brought in first, and then the walls were built? Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling all bore crisscrossing lines, dividing them into many large squares. Another thing that caught Qi Xia's attention was the goat-head's mention of "nine of you." No matter how you counted, ten people sat around the table, plus the goat-head himself, making eleven in the room. What did "nine" mean? He reached into his pocket and, unsurprisingly, found his phone gone. "There's no need to introduce yourself," a cold woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop this immediately. I suspect you've detained us for over twenty-four hours, which constitutes illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used against you." As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arms, as if being dirty bothered her more than being imprisoned. Her words sobered the others somewhat. Whoever this was, kidnapping ten people alone had already crossed the line of the law. "Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white lab coat interrupted the group's thoughts, slowly turning to the cold woman. "We just woke up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, but sharp. The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table. "The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of staying up late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours," she said.
