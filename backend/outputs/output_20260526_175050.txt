An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire in the center of the room, flickering with a dim glow.

A tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, was spreading and seeping through the room.

In the very center of the room stood a large round table, weathered and worn, with a small ornate clock on its surface, its intricate patterns ticking away.

Around the table sat ten individuals in varied attire, their clothes shabby and faces smudged with dust.

Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all deep in sleep.

Beside them stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-headed mask, his gaze piercing through the worn mask, watching them with keen interest.

The clock on the table chimed as the minute and hour hands both pointed to "twelve."

From a distant place beyond the room came the low toll of a bell.

At that same moment, the ten men and women around the round table slowly awoke.

As they regained consciousness, they looked around in confusion, then at each other with bewilderment.

None seemed to recall how they had come to be here.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I'm delighted to meet you here. You've been asleep before me for twelve hours."

The man's appearance was truly eerie, startling everyone under the dim light.

His mask seemed made from a real goat's head, with many hairs yellowed and blackened, matted together.

Two hollows were cut for eyes, revealing his cunning gaze.

His every movement exuded not only the goat's unique musky scent but also a faint odor of decay.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before realizing the absurdity, hesitantly asking the goat-head, "Who... are you?"

"I'm sure you all have that question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands excitedly, as if he had long prepared an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head, quickly surveying the room. After a moment, his expression grew grave.

Strange—the room was too strange.

There were no doors, only walls on all sides.

In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were all sealed, with only a table in the center.

So how had they gotten here?

Had they been brought in first, then the walls built?

Qi Xia looked around again. Every surface—floor, walls, ceiling—was crisscrossed with lines, dividing them into large squares.

Another thing that bothered Qi Xia was the goat-head's mention of "nine of you."

No matter how he counted, there were ten people around the table, plus the goat-head, making eleven in total.

What did "nine" mean?

He reached into his pocket; as expected, his phone was gone.

"You don't need to introduce yourself," a cold woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop immediately. I suspect you've detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used against you."

As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arm, as if being dirty bothered her more than being imprisoned.

Her words sobered the others. Whoever this was, kidnapping ten people alone had already crossed legal boundaries.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted, looking at the cold woman. "We've just woken up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady but sharp.

The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table. "The clock shows twelve, but I'm a late sleeper. When I last checked the time at home, it was already twelve. That means we've been detained for at least twelve hours."

She finished speaking.
