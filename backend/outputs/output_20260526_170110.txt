An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire in the center of the room, flickering with a dim glow. The tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, was spreading and permeating through the space. In the very middle of the room stood a large, worn-out round table, its surface scarred and battered. At the table’s center sat a small, intricately patterned clock, ticking away relentlessly.

Around the table sat ten individuals, each dressed in shabby attire, their faces smeared with dust. Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all lost in deep slumber. Beside them stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-head mask. His gaze pierced through the worn mask, fixed intently on the ten sleepers.

The clock on the table chimed as the minute and hour hands simultaneously pointed to “twelve.” From somewhere far beyond the room came a low, resonant bell toll. At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awakened. As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in confusion, then exchanged puzzled glances. Clearly, none remembered how they had come to be here.

“Good morning, nine of you,” the goat-head spoke first. “I’m delighted to meet you here. You have been sleeping before me for twelve hours.” The man’s attire was utterly bizarre, startling everyone in the dim light. His mask appeared to be made from a real goat’s head, with much of the fur yellowed, blackened, matted, and tangled. Two hollows were cut out for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze. Every gesture he made exuded not only the distinct musky odor of a goat but also a faint scent of decay.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally grasping the absurdity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the goat-head, “You… who are you?”

“I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you,” the goat-head said, waving his hands excitedly, as if he had long prepared his answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and after a moment, his expression grew grave. Strange—this room was utterly bizarre. There were no doors; walls surrounded it on all sides. In other words, the room’s walls, ceiling, and floor were all sealed, with only a table placed in the center. How, then, had they come to be here? Could they have been brought in first, with the walls built afterward?

Qi Xia looked around again. Whether it was the floor, walls, or ceiling, all were crisscrossed by lines, dividing the surfaces into large squares. Another thing that caught his attention was the goat-head’s mention of “nine of you.” No matter how one counted, there were ten people seated around the round table, plus the goat-head himself, making eleven in the room. What did “nine of you” mean?

He reached into his pocket and, as expected, found his phone had been taken away.

“There’s no need to introduce yourself,” a cold, clear-voiced woman said to the goat-head. “I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you’ve detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting the crime of unlawful detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used as evidence against you.” As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arms, as if she disliked being dirtied more than being imprisoned.

Her words sobered the others considerably. Whoever this person was, daring to kidnap ten people alone had already crossed the line of the law.

“Wait…” a middle-aged man in a white lab coat interrupted the train of thought. He looked slowly at the cold-voiced woman and asked, “We’ve all just woken up. How do you know we’ve been detained for twenty-four hours?” His tone was steady and forceful, yet incisive.

The woman pointed calmly at the clock on the table and replied, “The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of sleeping late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we’ve been detained for at least twelve hours.” After speaking, she folded her arms.
