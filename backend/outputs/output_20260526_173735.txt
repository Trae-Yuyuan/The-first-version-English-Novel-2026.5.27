An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire in the center of the room, casting a dim, flickering glow.

A quiet atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, was spreading and seeping through the room.

In the very center of the room stood a large, round table, which appeared worn and mottled. At the center of the table sat a small desk clock, intricately patterned, ticking away.

Around the table sat ten individuals of varied attire. Their clothes looked somewhat shabby, and their faces were stained with dust.

Some were slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all fast asleep.

Beside these ten people stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-head mask.

His gaze pierced through the worn goat-head mask, fixing intently on the ten with keen interest.

The clock on the table chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing to "twelve."

From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled.

At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awakened.

As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in confusion, then glanced at each other with bewilderment.

It seemed no one remembered how they had come to be there.

"Good morning, nine of you," the Goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have been sleeping before me for twelve hours."

The man's attire was truly bizarre, startling everyone under the dim light.

His mask appeared to be made from a real goat's head, with much of its hair yellowed and blackened, matted and clumped together.

Two hollows were cut out at the eyes of the goat mask, revealing his cunning eyes.

Every gesture he made not only emitted the unique muttony smell of a goat but also a faint, rotting odor.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally realizing the absurdity of the situation. He hesitantly asked the Goat-head, "You... who are you?"

"I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the Goat-head said, waving his hands excitedly, as if he had long prepared an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the Goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and moments later, his expression grew grave.

Strange. This room was truly strange.

There were no doors, only walls on all sides.

In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were all sealed, yet a table sat in the center.

If so, how had they arrived here?

Had they been brought in first, and then the walls were built around them?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling all bore crisscrossing lines, dividing the surfaces into many large squares.

Another thing that bothered Qi Xia was the Goat-head's phrase "nine of you."

No matter how he counted, ten people sat around the round table. Including the Goat-head himself, there were eleven in the room.

What did "nine" mean?

He reached into his pocket and, as expected, found his phone had been taken.

"There's no need for introductions," a cold woman said to the Goat-head. "I advise you to cease your actions immediately. I suspect you've detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting the crime of illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and could become unfavorable testimony against you."

As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arms, as if she hated being dirtied more than being imprisoned.

The cold woman's words sobered the group considerably. Whoever he was, kidnapping ten people alone had already crossed the legal line.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted everyone's train of thought. He looked slowly at the cold woman and asked, "We've all just woken up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and firm, but struck to the point.

The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table and replied, "The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of staying up late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours."

Having said that, she fell silent.
