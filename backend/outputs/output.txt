An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire at the center of the room, flickering with a dim glow. The tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, was spreading and diffusing throughout the space.

At the very center of the room stood a large, round table, its surface mottled and worn. On the table sat a small mantel clock, its intricate patterns ornate, ticking away steadily. Around the table sat ten individuals, each dressed in varied attire that appeared somewhat shabby, their faces smudged with dust. Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all sound asleep.

Beside these ten people stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat’s head mask. His gaze pierced through the worn mask, fixed intently on the sleeping group. The clock on the table chimed as both the minute and hour hands aligned at “twelve.” From a distant place beyond the room, a low, resonant bell echoed.

At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table gradually awoke. As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in bewilderment, then exchanged puzzled glances. It seemed none of them remembered how they had come to be here.

“Good morning, nine of you,” the goat-head spoke first. “I’m delighted to meet you here. You have been asleep before me for twelve hours.” The man’s bizarre attire, illuminated by the dim light, startled everyone. His mask seemed to be made from a real goat’s head, with much of its hair yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled together. Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze. His every movement exuded not only the unique smell of a goat but also a faint, rotting odor.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally grasping the absurdity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the goat-head, “Who… are you?”

“I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you,” the goat-head said, waving his hands excitedly, as if he had long prepared his answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and after a moment, his expression grew serious. Strange—the room was exceedingly strange. There was no door; all four walls were solid. In other words, the room’s walls, ceiling, and floor were sealed, with only a table placed at its center. If so, how had they gotten in? Could it be that they were brought here first, and then the walls were built afterward?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling all bore crisscrossing lines, dividing the surfaces into many large squares. Another thing that caught his attention was the goat-head’s reference to “nine of you.” No matter how you counted, there were ten people seated around the table, and including the goat-head himself, there were eleven in the room. What did “nine” mean?

He reached into his pocket and, unsurprisingly, found his phone had been taken away.

“There’s no need to introduce yourself,” a cold woman said to the goat-head. “I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you have detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used as evidence against you.” As she spoke, she grimaced and rubbed the dust off her arms, seeming more annoyed by the dirt than by being imprisoned.

The cold woman’s words sobered the others. No matter who this person was, kidnapping ten individuals alone had already crossed the legal boundary.

“Wait…” a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted the train of thought. He slowly looked at the cold woman and asked, “We all just woke up. How do you know we’ve been detained for twenty-four hours?” His tone was steady and forceful, but it cut to the point.

The cold woman calmly pointed to the mantel clock on the table and replied, “The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of sleeping late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. That means we’ve been detained for at least twelve hours.” After saying this, she fell silent.
