An aged tungsten filament lamp, suspended from a black wire in the center of the room, flickered with a dim glow.

The tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, began to diffuse and spread throughout the room.

At the heart of the room stood a large, round table, its surface mottled and worn. At its center sat a small, intricately patterned desk clock, ticking softly.

Around the table sat ten individuals, each dressed in different attire. Their clothes appeared somewhat shabby, and their faces were stained with dust.

Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all of them fast asleep.

Beside these ten, stood a man silently. He wore a goat-headed mask and a black suit.

His gaze pierced through the dilapidated goat mask, fixing intently on the ten with keen interest.

The clock on the table chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing to "twelve."

From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled.

At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly stirred awake.

As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in bewilderment, then exchanged puzzled glances.

Apparently, none of them remembered how they had come to be here.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have been sleeping before me for twelve hours."

The man's attire was indeed bizarre, startling everyone in the dim light.

His mask seemed to be made from a real goat's head, much of its fur yellowed and blackened, matted and stuck together.

Two hollow holes were cut out for the eyes, revealing his shrewd gaze.

Every gesture he made not only emitted the distinct musky scent of a goat but also carried a faint odor of decay.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally realizing the absurdity of the situation. He asked the goat-head hesitantly, "Who... are you?"

"I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you." The goat-head waved his hands excitedly, as if he had long prepared his answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and moments later, his expression turned grave.

Strange. This room was truly strange.

There was no door. All four walls were solid.

In other words, the room—its walls, ceiling, and floor—were completely sealed, with a table placed oddly at its center.

If so, how had they gotten here?

Had someone brought them in first, then built the walls afterward?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling alike were crisscrossed with lines, dividing the surfaces into many large squares.

Another thing that troubled Qi Xia was the goat-head's use of "nine of you."

No matter how you counted, there were ten people seated around the round table. Including the goat-head himself, there were eleven in the room.

What did "nine of you" mean?

He reached into his pocket and, unsurprisingly, found his phone gone.

"There's no need for introductions," a cold woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you have detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting the crime of illegal detention. Everything you say now will be recorded and used as incriminating evidence against you."

As she spoke, she disdainfully rubbed the dust off her arm, as if she hated being dirty more than being imprisoned.

The cold woman's words sobered the group considerably. Whoever this person was, daring to kidnap ten people alone had already crossed a legal line.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted everyone's train of thought. He turned slowly to the cold woman and asked, "We all just woke up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, but sharp.

The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table and replied, "The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of sleeping late. When I last checked the time at home, it was already twelve. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours."
