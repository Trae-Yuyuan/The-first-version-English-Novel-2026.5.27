An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire in the center of the room, casting a dim glow. A serene atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, was spreading and diffusing through the space. In the very middle of the room stood a large round table, worn and battered, with a small ornate clock at its center, its intricate patterns ticking away. Around the table sat ten people of varying attire, their clothes somewhat shabby and their faces smudged with dust. Some were slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all fast asleep. Beside these ten people stood a man silently, wearing a goat-headed mask and a black suit. His gaze pierced through the worn goat mask, fixing intently on the ten with keen interest. The clock on the table chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing to twelve. From a distant place outside the room came a low, resonant bell toll. At the same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awakened. As they regained consciousness, they first looked around in bewilderment, then exchanged puzzled glances. It seemed no one remembered how they had ended up here. "Good morning, nine," the Goat Head spoke first. "I'm delighted to meet you all here. You have slept before me for twelve hours." The man's outfit was truly bizarre, startling everyone under the dim light. His mask seemed made from a real goat's head, its fur yellowed and darkened, matted and tangled. Two hollow holes were cut where the eyes would be, revealing his cunning eyes. Every gesture he made not only emitted the unique rancid smell of a goat but also a faint, decaying odor. A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before finally realizing the absurdity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the Goat Head, "You... who are you?" "I believe you all share that question, so I'll introduce myself to the nine," the Goat Head said, waving his hands joyfully, as if he had long prepared an answer. A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the Goat Head. He quickly surveyed the room, and moments later, his expression grew grave. Strange, this room was too strange. There was no door, only walls on all sides. In other words, the room's walls, ceiling, and floor were all sealed, yet a table sat in the center. How, then, had they gotten here? Could it be that they were brought in first, and then the walls were built? Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling all had crisscrossing lines, dividing the surfaces into large squares. Another thing that caught Qi Xia's attention was the Goat Head's mention of "nine." No matter how you counted, there were ten people seated around the table, plus the Goat Head himself, making eleven in the room. What did "nine" mean? He reached into his pocket and, as expected, found his phone had been taken. "There's no need to introduce yourself to us," a cold woman said to the Goat Head. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you've detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting illegal imprisonment. Everything you say now will be recorded and used as evidence against you." As she spoke, she rubbed the dust off her arms with disdain, as if being dirty bothered her more than being imprisoned. The cold woman's words indeed jolted everyone awake. No matter who this person was, daring to kidnap ten people alone had already crossed the line of the law. "Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted everyone's thoughts, slowly looking at the cold woman. He asked, "We've all just woken up. How do you know we've been imprisoned for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, but hit the nail on the head. The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table and replied, "The clock here shows twelve o'clock, but I have a habit of staying up late. The last time I checked the clock at home, it was already twelve. That means we've been imprisoned for at least twelve hours." She finished speaking.
