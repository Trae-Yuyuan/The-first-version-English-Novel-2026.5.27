An old tungsten filament lamp hung from the ceiling by a black wire, casting a dim, flickering glow.

The tranquil atmosphere, like ink dripping into clear water, spread and permeated the room.

At the center of the room stood a large, round table, worn and mottled with age. In the middle of the table sat a small, ornate clock, its intricate patterns ticking away the seconds.

Seated around the table were ten individuals, each dressed in shabby clothes, their faces smudged with dust. Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all in a deep slumber.

Beside these ten stood a man in a black suit, wearing a goat-head mask. His gaze pierced through the worn mask, fixed intently on the sleeping figures.

The clock on the table chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing to "twelve."

From somewhere far beyond the room, a deep, resonant bell tolled.

At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awoke. As they regained consciousness, they looked around in confusion, then exchanged puzzled glances with one another.

None of them seemed to remember how they had come to be there.

"Good morning, nine of you," the Goathead spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have slept before me for twelve hours."

The man’s attire was bizarre, startling everyone in the dim light. His mask seemed made from a real goat’s head, its fur yellowed and matted, clumped together. Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze.

Every gesture he made exuded not only the distinct musky smell of a goat but also a faint, rotting odor.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before realizing the absurdity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the Goathead, "Who... are you?"

"I’m sure you all have that question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the Goathead said, waving his hands with delight, as if he had long prepared an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the Goathead. He quickly surveyed the room, and moments later, his expression grew grave.

Strange. This room was too strange. There were no doors, only walls on all sides. In other words, the room, its ceiling, and floor were entirely sealed, with a table placed inexplicably in the center.

How, then, had they gotten here? Could they have been brought in first, and the walls built afterward?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling were all crisscrossed with lines, dividing the surfaces into large squares.

Another thing that bothered Qi Xia was the Goathead’s mention of "nine of you." No matter how he counted, there were ten people seated around the table. Including the Goathead himself, that made eleven in the room.

What did "nine" mean?

He reached into his pocket and found, as expected, that his phone had been taken.

"You don’t need to introduce yourself," a cold, clear-voiced woman said to the Goathead. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you’ve detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting illegal imprisonment. Every word you say now will be recorded and used as evidence against you."

As she spoke, she rubbed the dust from her arms with disdain, as if she hated being dirty more than being imprisoned.

Her words sobered the group. Whoever this was, daring to kidnap ten people alone had already crossed the line of the law.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted the others. He looked slowly at the cold woman and asked, "We’ve all just woken up. How do you know we’ve been imprisoned for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, cutting straight to the point.

The cold woman pointed calmly at the clock on the table. "The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of staying up late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. That means we’ve been imprisoned for at least twelve hours."

She finished speaking.
