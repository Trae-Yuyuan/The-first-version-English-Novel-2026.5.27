An old tungsten filament lamp hung from a black wire at the center of the room, casting a dim, flickering glow. The tranquil atmosphere spread through the space like ink dripping into clear water.

At the heart of the room stood a large round table, weathered and worn, with a small, intricately patterned clock at its center, ticking away. Seated around the table were ten individuals, each dressed differently, their clothes somewhat shabby and their faces smudged with dust. Some slumped over the table, others leaned back in their chairs, all of them fast asleep.

Beside these ten people stood a man silently, wearing a goat-head mask and a black suit. His gaze pierced through the worn mask, fixated on the ten with keen interest.

The clock on the table chimed, its minute and hour hands both pointing to "twelve." From a distant place beyond the room, a low bell tolled.

At that same moment, the ten men and women seated around the round table slowly awoke. As they regained clarity, they first looked around in confusion, then exchanged puzzled glances. None seemed to recall how they had come to be there.

"Good morning, nine of you," the goat-head spoke first. "I am delighted to meet you here. You have been asleep before me for twelve hours."

The man's attire was eerie, startling everyone under the dim light. His mask seemed made from a real goat's head, its fur yellowed and blackened, matted and tangled. Two hollow holes were cut for the eyes, revealing his cunning gaze. Every gesture he made exuded not only the goat's characteristic smell but also a faint, decaying odor.

A man with tattooed arms froze for a few seconds before realizing the oddity of the situation. Hesitantly, he asked the goat-head, "Who... are you?"

"I believe you all share this question, so let me introduce myself to the nine of you," the goat-head said, waving his hands excitedly, as if he had long prepared an answer.

A young man named Qi Xia sat farthest from the goat-head. He quickly surveyed the room, and moments later, his expression turned grave. Strange—this room was too strange. There were no doors, only walls on all sides. In other words, the room's sides, ceiling, and floor were sealed, with a table placed squarely in the center. If so, how had they arrived here? Could they have been brought in first, and then the walls were built?

Qi Xia looked around again. The floor, walls, and ceiling all bore crisscrossing lines, dividing them into large squares. Another thing that troubled him was the goat-head's mention of "nine of you." No matter how he counted, there were ten people seated around the table. Including the goat-head himself, there were eleven in the room. What did "nine of you" mean?

He reached into his pocket; as expected, his phone was gone.

"There is no need to introduce yourself," a cold woman said to the goat-head. "I advise you to stop your actions immediately. I suspect you have detained us for over twenty-four hours, constituting illegal detention. Every word you say now will be recorded and used against you." As she spoke, she rubbed the dust off her arms with disgust, as if being dirty bothered her more than being imprisoned.

Her words sobered the others. No matter who this was, kidnapping ten people alone crossed the line of the law.

"Wait..." a middle-aged man in a white coat interrupted the train of thought. He looked slowly at the cold woman and asked, "We all just woke up. How do you know we've been detained for twenty-four hours?" His tone was steady and forceful, but sharp.

The cold woman calmly pointed to the clock on the table and replied, "The clock here points to twelve, but I have a habit of staying up late. The last time I checked my watch at home, it was already twelve. This means we've been detained for at least twelve hours."
